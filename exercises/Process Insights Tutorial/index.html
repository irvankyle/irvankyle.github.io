Introduction

How to Complete the Exercise in this Tutorial

This tutorial will teach you how to use Process HQ to add a new business process, identify process inefficiencies, create a process view, and create a custom KPI. Follow the steps in Exercise 1 to access Appian Community Edition, and then deploy the Order Management (OM) application from the app catalog.

How Can I Practice?
You should practice in Appian Community Edition. This free, community Appian resource comes with the ability to deploy pre-built Appian applications.  
Keep in mind that this is a community environment not suitable for production workloads or sensitive information.
Appian Version
Appian Community Edition is on the latest Appian version. If you are following the exercises from a previous Appian version, go to academy.appian.com to download the latest version.
Naming Conventions
When you register for Appian Community Edition, you gain access to a workspace, which is your personal area within the community environment. When your workspace has been assigned, you will receive a confirmation email with a workspace ID. Pre-loaded apps in your workspace include the workspace ID.
Use this workspace ID when creating new applications and objects. Otherwise, you could run into naming conflicts with objects created by other users. In the example below, the application prefix for the Acme Auto Solution app is W0000AS. So, the workspace ID is W0000, which would be included in any new app's prefix.

App Catalog
The App Catalog contains the most recent versions of pre-built apps available in Appian Community Edition. To go to the App Catalog, on Community Edition Home, click BROWSE APP CATALOG.
To determine if you have the latest app version, select the app in the list and review the last updated date. If this date is later than when you registered for Appian Community Edition, you should deploy a new version. If you recently received your workspace, you likely have the latest versions.
Save Often
Appian does not automatically save updates, so save your objects frequently.
Additional Resources
Appian provides a number of training resources for Appian developers. The following resources are particularly popular with our learners: 
Academy Online - Appian's online courses provide useful survey courses, step-by-step tutorials, and practice exercises. Explore these resources at your own pace. Survey courses will help you develop a better grasp of the topics you need to learn. Video and print tutorials will help you with getting hands-on experience with Appian.
Community Discussions for New Users - Check out the New to Appian thread in Community. Join our community of experts to ask questions and find answers from past discussions. 
Appian Documentation - Appian's product documentation will provide you with an overview of key Appian features, newest release information, additional tutorials, and helpful patterns and recipes to implement in your app.

Exercise 1: Access Appian Community Edition

The exercises are developed for Appian Community Edition. Once you register, you will have access to your own Appian workspace. The workspace comes with preloaded objects that you will need as you complete these steps.

Follow the steps below to register:

  1. Go to Appian Community Edition.
  2. Sign in with your Appian Community credentials.
  3. Select a region, then complete the onboarding questions and acknowledgement. When your workspace has been assigned, you’ll receive a confirmation email.

Keep in mind that this is a community environment not suitable for production workloads or sensitive information.

For help accessing this environment, please email CommunityEdition@appian.com.

To get the pre-built app used in these exercises, follow the steps below:

  1. From Community Edition Home, click BROWSE APP CATALOG.
  2. Select the Order Management,
  3. Click DEPLOY APP TO WORKSPACE then DEPLOY to deploy the application.

The deployment may take several minutes. When complete, you’ll receive a confirmation email, and the new app will appear in your workspace.

Scenario

In this exercise, you’ll act as a business analyst and start an investigation of the order fulfillment process.

You’ll start your exploration by starting a new investigation. Appian will assist by suggesting the attributes to investigate further. Once you identify an area for improvement, you’ll create an insight to share across your team. Then, you will apply filters to create a view into a meaningful segment of your data. Finally, you’ll create a KPI for tracking a specific process metric.

Explore the Process

  1. Navigate to the Process Insights page in Process HQ, and open the process you just created. \

>>>>> gd2md-html alert: inline image link here (to images/image15.png). Store image on your image server and adjust path/filename/extension if necessary.
(Back to top)(Next alert)
>>>>>

  1. Spend a few minutes on the SUMMARY tab reviewing the high-level generated discoveries, so that you can understand your process better before you dig deeper. As a business analyst, here you can accomplish the following:
    • Review the default KPIs.
    • Review the process diagram to see how the process runs in practice.
    • Review the available case attributes.
    • Review the default chart that reflects the change in volume and duration over time.
    • Adjust the timeframe for displayed data.
  2. Try to answer the following questions?
    • Look at the process diagram. Which activities, if any, appear to be slowing down the process? Which sequence in the process takes the longest?
    • Look at the chart at the bottom of the SUMMARY tab. What happens to case duration as the number of cases increases?

Best Practice

A typical insight should have 2 to 3 findings, but this number will vary based on your dataset. Include as many findings as you need to provide the right level of detail for your use case.

In this exercise, you added two findings. However, a business analyst could continue with the investigation by looking at additional attributes in the process.

**Congratulations! **

**You just saved an investigation with a new and valuable insight. You or other analysts can add more insights to this investigation later. **

Create a View

The investigation in the previous step revealed a bottleneck involving repeated work with the shipping vendor Pioneer Solutions. Following this investigation, analysts were able to look deeper into affected processes and determine that errors in manual data entry are causing the delays. With this issue addressed, you will now create a view for your process. Views show metrics for a filtered subset of your process data. In this case, you’ll create a view that allows you to monitor cases involving Pioneer Solutions to assess performance in the future.

  1. Navigate to the SUMMARY tab.
    • If the SUMMARY tab is not visible, you may need to navigate back to your process using the breadcrumbs at the top of the page.
  2. Open the ALL CASES dropdown and click CREATE VIEW.

Create a KPI

As you create views and conduct investigations, Appian provides you with default KPIs. However, there will be times when you want to create custom KPIs that reflect your own understanding of a business process. In this exercise, you’ll build a custom KPI to monitor the duration of the Notified the Shipping Vendor Activity and add it to the view created in the previous step. Tracking this KPI over time allows you to see at a glance if the issue at Pioneer Solutions is being appropriately addressed.

  1. Verify that you are in the view you just created: Orders Shipped via Pioneer Solutions.

  1. Click CREATE NEW KPI.
  2. Click Duration then NEXT.

  3. In the Create KPI dialog, select Activity Duration, and then apply the following configurations:

    • Activity: Select Notified the Shipping Vendor.
    • Aggregation: Select Average.
    • Unit: Select Detect automatically.

    You’ll see the preview of your KPI on the right:

  2. Click CREATE. Your KPI now displays in the CUSTOM KPIs tab.
